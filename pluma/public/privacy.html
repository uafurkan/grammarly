Privacy Policy

Last updated: 13 June 2026

Pluma is built on one promise: your writing is yours. It never leaves your device to be analysed, and there is no account to create and no profile to build.

The short version. Pluma has no servers that see your text, no analytics, no advertising, and no tracking. Everything you write is checked on your own device — in your browser or inside Word — and stored only on that device until you remove it.

What we collect

Nothing. Pluma does not have a backend that receives your documents. We do not collect your name, email, IP address, location, or any usage statistics. There are no cookies used for tracking, no fingerprinting, and no third-party advertising or analytics scripts.

Where your text is processed

All grammar, spelling, clarity, tone, and readability checking runs entirely on your device. The dictionaries and rules are downloaded once and cached locally; from then on, checking works even with no internet connection. The same is true of the Word add-in: your document text is read by the add-in in Word's own task pane and processed locally — it is never uploaded to us.

Where your work is stored

Documents you create or open are saved locally in your browser using its built-in storage (localStorage and IndexedDB). They stay on your device until you delete them or clear your browser data. We cannot see them, and they are not synced to any server. If you want a backup or to move your work to another device, you can export it to a file yourself and import it elsewhere — that transfer happens directly through your own file, not through us.

Optional features that use the internet

A few features reach out to free, public services only when you choose to use them. When you do, your query — not your full document — is sent to that service, and its own privacy policy applies:

  • Find sources / citations. Searching academic databases or looking up a DOI queries the free OpenAlex and Crossref APIs with your search terms.
  • Synonyms & suggestions. The optional thesaurus and word suggestions query the free Datamuse API with the single word or short phrase you are working on.

If you are offline, these features fall back gracefully and Pluma keeps working. None of them send your whole document anywhere.

On-device AI

Pluma's optional AI assistance runs a small language model inside your browser (via WebGPU). The model is downloaded once and cached; after that, rewriting and other AI help happen locally. Your text is not sent to any AI server.

Children's privacy

Pluma collects no personal information from anyone, including children. Because there is no account and no data collection, there is nothing to gather about users of any age.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change with it. Material changes will be reflected on this page.
